Muhammad Ali

The Greatest of All Time

Here's the timeline of Ali's boxing career.

  • 1954 - Amateur boxing debut against Ronnie O’Keefe, won by split decision.
  • 1960 - Won the Light-heavyweight Gold in Summer Olympics in Rome and started his professional boxing career winning against Tunney Hunsaker.
  • 1964 - Won the World Heavyweight Title against Sonny Liston by TKO and changed his name to Muhammad Ali.
  • 1965 - Rematch with Sonny Liston, won the match by KO at first round.
  • 1966 - Ali refused to serve the military and was denied his boxing license the reason he stopped fighting on the boxing ring.
  • 1970 - Return of Ali, fought Jerry Quarry and won the match.
  • 1971 - Lost the “Fight of the Century” against Joe Frazier.
  • 1972 - Won his fights against Jerry Quarry, Floyd Patterson and Bob Foster.
  • 1973 - Faced Ken Norton and lost the fight.
  • 1974 - Won the rematch with Joe Frazier and came out victorious against heavyweight champion George Foreman in the fight dubbed as “The Rumble in the Jungle”.
  • 1975 - The third fight of Ali and Frazier dubbed as the “Thrilla in Manila”. Ali won by TKO.
  • 1980 - Suffered a loss from Larry Holmes.
  • 1981 - Lost to Trevor Berbick and was his final boxing match.
